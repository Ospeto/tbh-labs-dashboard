## Executive Summary

TBH Labs Myanmar is a Myanmar-language tech channel that has grown from 1 million views (Dec 2021) to over **64 million cumulative views** in roughly four years ‚Äî crossing the 50 million mark in April 2025. The channel publishes an average of **13.8 videos per month**, with a median per-video view count of **55,686** and an average of **87,565**.

The channel is currently in a strategic transition: shifting heavily toward **Knowledge** content (67.6% of 2026 uploads) while scaling back **Showcases** and short-form content. Engagement is at an all-time high (5.35% like rate in Q1 2026), suggesting the audience deeply values the new direction ‚Äî though per-video view counts have dipped as the channel absorbs this strategic shift.

This report breaks down every dimension of the channel's performance and concludes with actionable recommendations.

## 2. Duration Analysis ‚Äî What Length Performs Best?

**Average video duration:** 11 minutes 38 seconds

| Duration Bucket | Videos | Avg Views | Median Views | Avg Likes | Avg Comments |
|-----------------|-------:|----------:|-------------:|----------:|-------------:|
| 0‚Äì3 min | 60 | 15,818 | 9,455 | 780 | 13 |
| 3‚Äì5 min | 11 | 29,698 | 16,164 | 1,531 | 66 |
| 5‚Äì8 min | 176 | 59,875 | 42,461 | 2,299 | 108 |
| 8‚Äì10 min | 132 | 73,482 | 40,952 | 2,610 | 115 |
| 10‚Äì15 min | 210 | 96,081 | 68,432 | 4,135 | 193 |
| **15‚Äì20 min** | **89** | **148,119** | **121,128** | **5,708** | **288** |
| **20‚Äì30 min** | **40** | **162,157** | **138,398** | **6,506** | **346** |
| **30+ min** | **16** | **182,095** | **119,037** | **7,113** | **383** |

### Key Finding

There is a **near-linear relationship** between video length and performance. Longer videos don't just get more views ‚Äî they get *disproportionately more* engagement (likes and comments). The 15‚Äì20 minute bucket is the **optimal sweet spot**: it delivers 2.5√ó the views of the channel average with a robust sample size (89 videos).

The 30+ minute videos technically have the highest average (182K), but the sample size is small (16 videos) and the median drops to 119K ‚Äî suggesting a few outlier hits inflate the average. Meanwhile, 15‚Äì20 minute videos are more *consistently* high performers (median of 121K).

**Why this matters:** YouTube's algorithm rewards watch time. Longer videos that retain viewers signal high quality, leading to more recommendations. The data confirms this dynamic at TBH Labs ‚Äî investing more production time into 15‚Äì20 minute deep dives would reliably double view counts compared to the current channel average.

## 6. Expert Recommendations

### ‚úÖ What's Working ‚Äî Double Down

1. **Knowledge is the growth engine.** At 128K avg views, 4.9% like rate, and 67.6% of 2026 content, this is already the channel's strategic bet. The data validates it. Continue increasing the share. Aim for Knowledge to be 50‚Äì60% of uploads at steady state, leaving room for complementary categories.

2. **15‚Äì20 minute videos are the ideal length.** This is the sweet spot of high views (148K avg), strong engagement, and production feasibility. Target most flagship content at this length. Use 10‚Äì15 min for lighter topics and reserve 20‚Äì30 min for deep-dive tentpoles.

3. **Tuesday at 8:30 PM MMT is the golden upload slot.** Videos uploaded at this time average 171K views ‚Äî nearly 2√ó the channel average. Make this the primary upload window for all flagship content.

4. **Q4 is your cash cow.** September‚ÄìDecember consistently outperforms. Plan your biggest productions, collaborations, and tentpole videos for this window. Build a Q4 content calendar 2 months in advance.

### ‚ö†Ô∏è What Needs Attention

5. **Reduce Showcases output.** This category represents 28% of all content but delivers 39% below average views. Either transform Showcases into a more distinctive format (e.g., combine with narrative storytelling) or cut volume by 50% and redirect that production effort into Knowledge or Comparison content.

6. **Kill or rethink Shorts.** At 7,500 avg views, Shorts are the single worst-performing format. In 2025, 13.8% of uploads were Shorts ‚Äî a significant production investment with minimal return. Shorts rarely convert to long-form subscribers on tech channels because the audience intent is fundamentally different. Unless there's a specific growth experiment, **stop producing Shorts entirely** and reallocate that effort.

7. **Scale the hidden gems:**
   - **Comparison** (7 videos, 158K avg) ‚Äî highly underproduced. Comparison videos are evergreen, search-friendly, and naturally long-form. Produce 2‚Äì3 per month.
   - **Unboxing** (11 videos, 151K avg) ‚Äî another underproduced winner with consistent performance (median 150K). These are also the most shareable format on social media.
   - **Company Profiles** (3 videos, 133K avg) ‚Äî narrative-driven format with strong engagement (4.5% like rate). Even 1 per month would significantly uplift the channel average.

8. **Address the "uncategorized" 120 videos.** These represent 16% of all content sitting outside the playlist system. Many of these are strong performers (several 400K+ hits). Categorizing them properly would improve channel organization, playlist recommendations, and session watch time.

### üéØ Strategic Growth Plays

9. **Create a "Flagship Friday" or "Knowledge Tuesday" brand.** The data shows Tuesday is the best upload day and Knowledge is the best category. Establishing a recurring branded series (e.g., "Knowledge Tuesday" at 8:30 PM MMT) creates audience habit, improves notification click-through rates, and signals consistency to the algorithm.

10. **Build series, not one-offs.** The top-performing categories (Knowledge, Company Profiles, Tech Talk) lend themselves to serialization. A series like "How Things Work" or "Tech Origins" would create playlist binge sessions that dramatically boost session watch time ‚Äî the #1 algorithm signal on YouTube.

11. **Exploit the September‚ÄìDecember window aggressively.** Plan 4‚Äì5 tentpole videos for this period:
    - September: Flagship phone launch coverage (iPhone, Pixel)
    - October: 1-month reviews and comparisons
    - November: Black Friday / buying guides
    - December: "Best of the Year" lists + deep-dive retrospective

12. **Experiment with 20‚Äì30 minute deep dives.** The data shows 20‚Äì30 min videos average 162K views ‚Äî well above the channel average ‚Äî but only 40 have been produced. With the audience's appetite for Knowledge content growing (67.6% of 2026 uploads), longer deep dives on topics like AI, semiconductors, and space tech are likely to perform even better than the historical average suggests.
